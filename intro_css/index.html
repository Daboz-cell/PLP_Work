Introduction

Kenyan cuisine is diverse and flavorful, influenced by different cultures, regions, and traditions. From coastal coconut-infused dishes to hearty meals from the highlands, food is a big part of Kenyan culture and hospitality.

Popular Kenyan Dishes

  • Ugali - A staple made from maize flour, often served with vegetables, meat, or fish.
  • Nyama Choma - Roasted meat, usually goat or beef, enjoyed with friends and family.
  • Chapati - Soft, layered flatbread, often served with stews or tea.
  • Pilau - Spiced rice dish cooked with meat or vegetables, popular at celebrations.
  • Mandazi - Fried dough snack, slightly sweet, enjoyed with tea.
  • Githeri - Boiled maize and beans, sometimes mixed with potatoes and vegetables.

Featured Recipe: Pilau

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 500g beef or goat meat (cubed)
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons Pilau masala
  • 4 cups beef stock or water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and fry onions until golden brown.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and Pilau masala. Stir for about a minute.
  3. Add meat and cook until browned.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until softened.
  5. Add rice and stir to coat with spices and oil.
  6. Pour in beef stock or water, add salt, and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until the rice is cooked and liquid absorbed.
  8. Serve hot with kachumbari (fresh tomato and onion salad).

Fun Facts About Kenyan Cuisine

  • Ugali is considered Kenya's unofficial national dish.
  • Kenya grows some of the world's best tea and coffee.
  • Coastal dishes often include coconut milk and spices from Indian and Arabic influence.
  • Street food like roasted maize, samosas, and bhajias are popular snacks.
